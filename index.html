Social Isolation faced by the Deaf

“Deafness is a community issue. When the Deaf are involved in the community, it helps the Deaf. When your community is involved with the Deaf, it helps your community. Don’t let inclusion be an illusion.”

 

There are about a million people in Singapore suffering from hearing impairment and half of them are deaf. However, studies show that many of them do not feel that they are socially included, accepted or given opportunities to achieve their potential due to a lack of understanding or awareness by the public.

Regardless of their disabilities, the Deaf have every right to full inclusion and participation in our society. Starting from today, let’s us all work towards including the Deaf in our workforce and social activities. Collectively, we can build a caring and inclusive society for them! ❤

Tips on interacting with the deaf

1. Get their attention before speaking them. Touch them lightly on the shoulder or wave your hand.

 

2. Face them when speaking. Some of them can read lips. In addition, important information cues can be obtained from your facial expressions and body language.

 

3. Other convenient methods of communication include WhatsApp messages, SMS, email or writing on paper. Remember, technology can help bring down barriers.

 

4. In a large group setting, take turns to speak one at a time. Keeping sentences short and succinct would be great!

 

5. Do ask them along for any gatherings and outings, they enjoy social and sports activities too!
